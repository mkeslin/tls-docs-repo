# Getting around

How to navigate Court Violations day to day.

## Open the module

From the main navigation, open **Court Violations**. Your access depends on agency permissions (view, modify, and report claims). If you cannot see a menu item, ask your agency administrator.

## Main areas

| Area | What it is for |
|------|----------------|
| **Dashboard** | Today’s agenda and links into work areas / queues |
| **Violation search** | Find cases by violation number, name, citation, filters |
| **Citation search** | Find source citations when you need to create or locate related court work |
| **Calendar** | Scheduled appearances, show-cause, pre-trial, and trial dates |
| **Bond search** | Find and manage bonds across cases |
| **Work queues** | Exception-driven lists (missed appearance, pending payment acceptance, collections eligible, and more) |
| **Reports** | Court reporting packs (OCA, DPS conviction, state quarterly, and related) |

Admin and setup areas (when your role includes them) may also include offense-to-fine mapping, speeding fine tables, OmniBase submissions, and health checks.

## Violation search results

Search results typically show the court violation number, violator, status indicators, and shortcuts into common actions. Favorites (pin/unpin), disposition-plan, and payment-plan indicators help you spot cases that already have programs or plans in place.

## Case detail

Opening a court violation shows the case record. Common tabs and panels include:

| Area | Typical contents |
|------|------------------|
| **General** | Parties, citation link, key identifiers, balances |
| **Status** | Current procedural state and available actions |
| **Offenses** | Charge / offense detail on the violation |
| **Attachments** | Documents and files |
| **History** | Timeline of events and prior actions |

Actions available on a case depend on its **current procedural state**. The action menu is grouped for readability (for example Scheduling, Plea & disposition, Compliance & financial, and Post-judgment & enforcement). Only enabled actions for that state appear.

## Tips

- Prefer **work queues** for “what needs attention,” and **search** when you already know a name or number.
- Use the **calendar** when planning a docket day, not only when looking up one defendant.
- If an action is disabled, the case usually fails a business rule (for example an active bond, plan, or missing date). Resolve the blocking condition, then try again.
