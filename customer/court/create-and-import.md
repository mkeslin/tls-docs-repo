# Create and import cases

How court violations enter the system and become active court work.

## How cases usually start

Most court violations are created from a **citation** issued in the field or entered in RMS. One citation can produce one or more court violations (for example multiple offenses).

Typical path:

1. Citation exists in Thin Line (or is imported).
2. Court violation record(s) are created for the court.
3. Clerk reviews the new case and **activates** it into the active court flow (commonly moving from **New** to **Pre-plea** with a first appearance date).

## New Case Review

New cases often appear in a **New Case Review** work queue. Use that queue to:

- Confirm the defendant and offense information
- Set or confirm the first appearance date
- Activate the case into Pre-plea
- Transfer or void when the case should not proceed in your court

Do not leave large backlogs in New without a plan — activated cases are what appear on the active docket and calendar workflows.

## Add a court violation

When your role allows it, you can **add** a court violation manually from the Court Violations module. Use this when a case must be entered without the usual citation path, following your agency’s policy.

## Import

Agencies that receive batches of court cases (for example during conversion or bulk intake) may use the **import** path. Import steps and file formats are agency-specific; coordinate with Thin Line during onboarding or conversion projects.

## After create / activate

Once a case is active:

- It can appear on the **calendar** when dates are set
- Clerks can take **plea and scheduling** actions from the case
- Payments, bonds, and programs become available according to state rules
