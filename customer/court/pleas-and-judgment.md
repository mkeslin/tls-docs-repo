# Pleas and judgment

Recording pleas, judgments, dismissals, transfers, and related disposition actions.

## Enter a plea

From **Pre-plea** (and related points where plea change is allowed):

1. Open the court violation.
2. Choose **Enter plea** (or **Change plea** when changing an existing plea).
3. Record the plea:

| Plea | Typical next state |
|------|--------------------|
| **Guilty** or **No contest** | Post-plea, pre-judgment |
| **Not guilty** | Pre-trial |

Appearance and scheduling dates may be required or updated as part of the action, depending on your court’s practice and the dialog.

## Enter judgment

After a guilty or no contest plea (or after a guilty verdict):

1. Choose **Enter judgment** (wording may specify guilty / no contest).
2. Complete judgment details required by the dialog.
3. The case typically moves to **Convicted**, where fines, compliance orders, and payment plans are handled.

From post-plea, the court may instead **grant a court program** rather than entering judgment immediately — see [Court programs](court-programs.md).

## Trial track (summary)

| Step | Typical action |
|------|----------------|
| Not guilty plea | Moves to Pre-trial |
| Set / update trial | Proceed to trial / update trial date |
| Guilty verdict | Convict — bench or jury → Verdict returned → Enter judgment |
| Not guilty verdict | Acquit — usually dismisses the case |
| Mistrial / reset | Return toward pre-trial |

## Dismiss, transfer, void, warning

| Action | When you typically use it |
|--------|---------------------------|
| **Dismiss case** | Court disposes without conviction (rules and fees vary by path) |
| **Transfer to another court** | Case belongs in a different court |
| **Void citation** | Citation should not proceed as a live case |
| **Warning** | Warning disposition path when used by your agency |

These actions are only available in states where the product allows them. Always follow your court’s legal procedures; the software enforces process steps, not legal advice.

## Reject or withdraw a plea

Use **Reject/withdraw plea** when the plea should not stand. The case generally returns toward **Pre-plea** so a new plea can be entered.

## Vacate judgment / appeal

When your role and state allow it:

- **Vacate judgment** reverses a judgment path (notes may be required).
- **File appeal** moves the case onto an appealed track.
