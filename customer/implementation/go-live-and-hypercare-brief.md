## What Thin Line will do

- Prepare and verify your Thin Line environment for production use  
- Complete agreed cutover steps with your project lead  
- Confirm critical workflows are usable before and immediately after cutover  
- Provide heightened support during the **hypercare** window named below  
- Help triage issues you report through the path in this brief  

Thin Line will **not** ask your staff to run internal engineering procedures. You will receive plain-language instructions from your project lead and Thin Line implementation lead.

---

## What your agency must do

- Follow the go-live window and any **data-entry freeze** instructions below  
- Ensure the go-live user group can log in and knows which system to use  
- Have supervisors available for the first shifts after go-live  
- Report problems promptly using the issue path below  
- Keep Thin Line informed if staffing, court, dispatch, or facility constraints change the plan

## What happens if go-live is delayed

- Thin Line and your acceptance authority may decide **No Go** or delay before cutover if readiness is incomplete.  
- Your project lead will tell staff which system to keep using.  
- Any freeze is lifted or adjusted in writing.  
- A new go-live window and updated brief will be issued.  

A delay is better than starting production use when staff cannot complete required work safely.

---

## What happens after hypercare

- Heightened cutover coverage ends on the planned hypercare end date (or when both sides agree it is complete).  
- Ongoing help uses Thin Line **Support** / your normal customer success path.  
- Open follow-ups remain tracked with owners; they do not disappear.  
- Future modules or enhancements are separate scope—not assumed in this go-live.
