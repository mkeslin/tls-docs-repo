# Unit status

Free-unit status on the Units panel (not the same as En Route / Arrived on a call).

## Open unit controls

1. Open [live CAD](live-cad-overview.md).
2. Find the unit on the **Units** panel (group by Status if helpful; Hide Inactive when you want a cleaner board).
3. Use the unit menu / status actions your build shows.

## Common statuses

Labels match the product buttons. Codes are stored behind the scenes.

| Status (typical label) | Meaning |
|------------------------|---------|
| **Available** / **Back in Vehicle** | Ready to take a call |
| **Inactive** | Not in service for assignment |
| **On-Call (Standby)** | Standby / on-call posture per agency use |
| **Break** | Out of vehicle — break |
| **Meal** | Out of vehicle — meal |
| **Other** | Out of vehicle — other |

Out-of-vehicle units often display as **UNAVAILABLE** on the card and are not drag-assignable until back in service.

## Duty assignment

When your agency maintains duty-assignment codes, units may also carry a **Duty Assignment**. Use the codes your agency configured.

## Manage Units

Users with CAD admin can open **Manage Units** from the Units panel to maintain the unit roster (create/edit units). Day-to-day dispatchers usually do not need this — escalate roster changes to your CAD administrator or Thin Line during implementation.

## Tips

- Put a unit **Available** before expecting drag-assign to work.
- Self-dispatch officers must be assigned to a CAD unit on their user/officer profile — see [Self-dispatch](self-dispatch.md).
- Historical unit status/duty logs: [CAD Records — Unit status and logs](records/unit-status-and-logs.md).
