# From incident property

![Evidence](images/rms-evidence-detail.png)

How incident **Property** relates to **Evidence**, and when to take property into custody.

## Property vs evidence

| Concept | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **Property** (incident Property tab) | Property involved in the case (stolen, damaged, recovered, etc.) — may never enter the property room |
| **Evidence** | Property that has been **taken into agency control** with an evidence number and custody history |

Not every property line becomes evidence. Follow your agency’s evidence intake policy.

## Paths from the incident

Common UI paths:

1. Add a **property** line only (no evidence).
2. **Add as Evidence** on an existing property line (promote into custody).
3. Use **Add Evidence** / the [Add evidence wizard](add-evidence-wizard.md) from the Evidence tab (create property and/or take into custody in one flow).

After promotion, the incident **Evidence** tab lists evidence numbers for the case. Day-to-day room inventory and audit live in the Evidence module.

## Tips

- Search **master property** before creating duplicates — see [Master property and evidence](master-property-and-evidence.md).
- Complete intake history (IN AGENCY CONTROL) when the item physically enters custody — see [Chain of custody](chain-of-custody.md).
- IBRS / case completeness may still need property lines even when items are not booked as evidence.
