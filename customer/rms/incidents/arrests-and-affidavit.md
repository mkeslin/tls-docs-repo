# Arrests and affidavit

Arrest information, Arrest Search, and suspect arrest affidavits.

## Arrest information (arrestee)

On an involved person with an **Arrestee** (or equivalent arrest) role, complete arrest fields such as:

| Field | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Arrested on** | Date/time of arrest |
| **Arresting officer** | Officer who made the arrest |
| **Arrest type** | Agency arrest type code |
| **Cleared by** | Clearance / arrest clearance context when shown |

Exact labels follow your agency codes. Incomplete arrest data often fails IBRS or supervisory review.

## Arrest Search

Use **Incidents → Arrest Search** to find arrests across incidents — see [Search incidents](search.md).

## Arrest affidavit (suspect)

On a **Suspect** role, authorized users can enter **Arrest Affidavit** / PC narrative text and use **Print Arrest Affidavit** when configured.

- Affidavit content should match your agency’s legal and supervisory standards.
- Print from the suspect / affidavit UI after the text is complete.
- Legacy help may say “PC Affidavit” on older person tabs — current UI uses **Involved** with affidavit on the **Suspect** context.

## Booked in Jail

Suspect / arrest contexts may include **Booked in Jail** (or similar) to reflect jail booking. For full jail intake, see [Jail](../../jail/README.md); the incident flag does not replace Jail Intake booking steps.

Walk the full handoff (PD vs facility agency): [Arrest to jail booking](../../getting-started/journeys/arrest-to-jail-booking.md).

## Tips

- Coordinate affidavit prints with your magistrate / prosecutor process.
- If Arrest Search is missing, you may have a DA read-only or limited role — ask your administrator.
- Changing arrest data after approval may require a new supplement — see [Workflow, versions, and approval](workflow-versions-and-approval.md).
