# Incidents

Customer guides for the **Incidents (INC)** module — creating and managing law-enforcement incident reports in Thin Line RMS, including involved persons, arrests, property/evidence links, workflow approval, and IBRS validation.

## What an incident is

An **incident** is the primary investigative / case report record: what happened, when and where, who was involved, what offenses apply, and how the case is cleared or submitted for state reporting. Incidents may start from a **CAD** call, from a related **citation**, or from a manual **Add** in RMS.

| Record | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Incident** (this guide) | Case report, supplements, arrests, property/evidence links, IBRS readiness |
| **Fire Incident** | Separate module (FIR) — not covered here |
| **CAD call sheet** | Dispatch record that can create or link to an incident |

## Who this guide is for

- Officers and records staff who write or finish incident reports
- Supervisors who approve supplements and case status
- Staff who validate IBRS readiness and associate related records
- Trainers preparing RMS report-writing sessions
