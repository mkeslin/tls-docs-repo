# Involved persons

Victims, suspects, arrestees, and organizations on the **Involved** tab.

## Add involved parties

1. Open **Involved** on the incident detail.
2. Add a **Person**, **Organization**, or **Unknown Suspect** as needed.
3. Prefer linking an existing **master person** or organization — see [Master records](../../getting-started/master-records.md).
4. Assign the correct **role** for this incident.

## Roles (common)

| Role | Typical use |
|------|-------------|
| **Victim** | Person or org harmed / complaining party |
| **Suspect** | Subject of the investigation |
| **Arrestee** | Person arrested in connection with the incident |
| **Other** | Witnesses and other involved parties per agency practice |

A person can appear with the role your agency records for that event. Arrest-specific fields appear in arrestee / suspect contexts — see [Arrests and affidavit](arrests-and-affidavit.md).

## Suspect–victim relationships

When the product requires it, record **suspect relationships** to victims (for IBRS and case clarity). Complete these before validation when offenses require them.

## Unknown suspect

Use **Unknown Suspect** when identity is not yet established. Update to a master person later when identified, following your agency’s correction process and workflow rules.

## Tips

- Search masters before creating duplicates.
- Keep roles accurate — wrong role breaks arrest affidavit and IBRS paths.
- Organizations (businesses) use the organization add path, not person, when the victim/suspect is an org.
