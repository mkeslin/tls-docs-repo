# Narratives and offenses

Writing the report narrative and recording offenses on the incident.

## Narratives

Open the **Narratives** tab to add or edit narrative sections your agency uses (initial report, supplements, specialty narratives as configured).

- Write clearly; supplements may lock prior narrative versions when workflow advances.
- Use the voice and structure your agency’s policy requires (first person, chronological, etc.).
- Save as you go — do not rely on a single save at the end of a long session.

## Offenses

Open the **Offenses** tab to add offense lines for the case.

| Concept | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **Offense** | Statute / agency offense on the incident |
| **Report to IBRS** | Include this offense in IBRS / state reporting when applicable |

Offense choices drive involved-person relationships, property rules, and IBRS validation. Add all reportable offenses before submitting for approval when possible.

## Tips

- Search [Offense Search](../../getting-started/header-and-user-menu.md) from the header if you need to look up codes outside the tab.
- “Report to IBRS” vs local-only offenses is an agency policy decision — ask records if unsure.
- After offenses change, re-run **Validate** before transmit — see [Print, attachments, history, and IBRS](print-attachments-history-ibrs.md).
